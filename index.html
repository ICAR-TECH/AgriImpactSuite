AIS: AgriImpactSuite

AIS: AgriImpactSuite is a GUI application developed for performing complex agricultural or economic calculations related to adoption rates, economic surplus, risk reduction benefits, and the impact of technology adoption on poverty reduction. This software is built using the Python language and utilizes libraries like Tkinter (for GUI), NumPy, Matplotlib, SciPy, and Pandas for mathematical operations, plotting, and data manipulation.

AIS: AgriImpactSuite Modules

  • Estimation of Adoption Rate: Calculates the adoption rate of a particular agricultural practice or technology over time, based on factors like the initial adoption rate, present adoption rate, maximum adoption rate, and year of inception of the technology.
  • Estimation of Economic Surplus: Computes economic benefits (surplus) based on factors such as base year price & quantity, yield change, cost change, and elasticity of supply and demand. It provides the option to estimate the consumer surplus, producer surplus, and total economic surplus generated from technology adoption.
  • Risk Reduction Benefit: Assesses how implementing certain agricultural practices could reduce risk, using parameters like the relative risk aversion coefficient and coefficients of variation under control and improved conditions.
  • Poverty Reduction Effect: Estimates the impact of agricultural improvements on poverty reduction in a specific area. It factors in parameters such as the agricultural GDP (AgGDP), elasticity of poverty, and the number of poor people in the domain area.

PARC: Present-year Adoption Rate Calculator

PARC is a sub-module of the AgrImpactSuite software designed to calculate the adoption rate of a specific agricultural technology or intervention for the present year. The PARC tool helps in estimating how agricultural technologies (such as improved seed varieties) are adopted in a given region over time.

The PARC sub-module calculates the present-year adoption rate of agricultural technology based on various inputs like seed rate, multiplication factor, and crop area. It allows users to input data, calculate the rate, and save results for analysis.

Key Features

  • Economic Surplus Analysis: Quantifies social benefits from agricultural technologies.
  • Accessible to Non-Economists: User-friendly interface and minimal technical requirements.
  • Practical Exercises: Built-in modules for hands-on learning with real-world data.
  • Real-Case Examples: Includes case studies on crop varietal impacts.
  • Policy Insights: Guides policymakers in interpreting research value.

Application Areas

  • Impact Assessments: Evaluate returns on research investments.
  • Policy Support: Provide evidence-based insights for funding decisions.
  • Resource Allocation: Identify research areas with maximum societal benefits.
  • Technology Evaluation: Measure adoption impacts of agricultural innovations.
